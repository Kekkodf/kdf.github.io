My Projects

During my career as student, I've worked on some projects. Here are listed some of them:

Twitter Bots Influence on the Russo-Ukrainian War
During the 2022 Italian General Elections

It started as a project for the course of Advanced Topics in Computer and Network Security, then,
in colaboration with my friend and colleague Luca Cosuti and under the supervision of
Prof. Mauro Conti and Dott. Pier Paolo Tricomi we implemented further analysis
to find the influence of Twitter bots on the 2022 Italian General Elections.
The project was divided into two parts:

  • Creation of the dataset using the Twitter API.
  • Bot detection in the Italian Political Scenario.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP) and graph modeling of tweets.

The project was developed using Python and the following libraries:

  • Matplotlib
  • Seaborn
  • Scipy
  • NLTK

At the end, our research project was accepted and published at the SocialSec 2023 conference.
You can find the paper in arXiv.

DREX: Denial of service, Reconnaissance and Exploits eXecutable

The project was developed during the course of Information Security in colaboration with my friend and colleague Luca Cosuti.
The project consisted in the configuration of a network of virtual machines, the scripting of several attacks and the implementation of possible countermeasures.
The project was divided into three parts:

  • Configuration of Virtual Machines and network segments.
  • Attacks scripting in the program DREX.
  • Palo-Alto Firewall configuration and SPLUNK SIEM/Machine Learning ToolKit

The attacks were implemented using Python and the following libraries:

  • Scapy
  • Requests

The full project is available on GitHub.

Model of a pandemic

During my batchelor degree, I've worked on a project about the spread of a virus in a population.
The model was based on the SIRD mathematical model. SIRD is an acronym which stands for:

  • Susceptible
  • Infected
  • Recovered
  • Dead

It was implemented in Matlab and it was used to simulate the spread of a pandemic in a population.
The model consisted in the resolution of a system of differential equations using Runge-Kutta method.
